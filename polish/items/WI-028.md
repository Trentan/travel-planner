## Problem

On mobile, the combination of the sticky menu bar, tab navigation, and city navigation consumes nearly 30-40% of the vertical viewport. This leaves very little room for viewing itinerary cards or budget tables.

## Before

Screenshot: [mde-01-home.png](../screenshots/before/mde-01-home.png)

Mode: MDE

Callout: Notice how little content is visible below the navigation stack.

## Proposed Fix

Implement a condensed chrome mode that triggers when the user scrolls down. Hide the menu bar and let the tab and city navigation move into the freed space. Show the menu bar again when scrolling up or returning to the top.

Proposal: [WI-028-proposal.png](../screenshots/after/WI-028-proposal.png)

## After

Latest review screenshot: [WI-028-after.png](../screenshots/after/WI-028-after.png)

As the user scrolls down, the top app menu collapses away and the sticky tab/city navigation occupies a much smaller stack. Scrolling back up restores the full menu.
